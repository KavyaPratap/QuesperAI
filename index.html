Built By A Teacher's Family

"We watched teachers spend their evenings formatting papers instead of spending time with family."

Hi, I'm Kavya Pratap Singh, a third-year Computer Science Engineering student specializing in AI and Machine Learning.

The idea behind Quesper didn't start in a computer lab. It started during the COVID-19 lockdown when I was in Class 10. My father, a high school mathematics teacher, would spend hours every evening formatting exams, matrix brackets, and algebra symbols. It was unpaid clerical work that stole hours away from our family.

Determined to fix this, I began building algorithms to compile math structures automatically. Once I learned Android and systems architecture, Quesper was born.

Our Mission: Teaching is a noble profession, not a typesetting job. Quesper returns those hours back to teachers so they can spend them with their loved ones.

Our Social Principles

Built On Empathy & Transparency

How we run Quesper to respect educators and protect their time.

🤝

Empowering Unsung Heroes

Private school teachers are heavily overworked and underpaid. Quesper was built from the ground up to return those unpaid clerical hours. You were hired to shape minds, not to master complex layout engines or typesetting software.

💎

Radical Transparency

We are completely transparent about AI costs. Every teacher receives 10 papers free with no strings attached. After that, we keep credit packs equivalent to the price of a simple evening snack to cover server compute fairly.

Preserving Veteran Wisdom

A senior teacher's handwritten notes are a work of art compiled over decades. We believe this wisdom should never remain hidden in desk drawers. Snap a photo, scan it, and instantly digitize your academic legacy.
